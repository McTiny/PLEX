Engineering Background

Who I Am

Physical security engineer, infrastructure designer, and builder of tools for the limited energy community.

I design physical security and ICT systems for complex environments: federal installations, hyperscale data centers, airports, hospitals, correctional facilities, and enterprise campuses. My orientation is preventive: the most important engineering happens before systems are built, not after they fail. My work spans design development, construction documentation, design-assist delivery, and the translation of operational security requirements into systems that get built and perform as intended.

I hold the RCDD credential from BICSI, which validates expertise in ICT distribution design, structured cabling, and communications infrastructure. I serve on the BICSI Educational Technology Standards Committee, where I contribute to the standards that govern the field I work in.

Outside of design work, I build tools. The free tools on this site, PLEX and The Estimator, came from problems I actually needed to solve. I share them because the limited energy community deserves better instruments than the ones that currently exist.

Service

Military Background

U.S. Navy veteran. Army National Guard veteran.

I served in the U.S. Navy and subsequently in the Army National Guard. Military service shaped how I approach engineering work: methodically, with attention to consequence, and with an understanding that systems have to work under pressure, not just under ideal conditions. That orientation carries into every design I produce and every tool I build. The discipline of planning for degraded operations (maintaining capability when normal conditions fail) is not a military concept. It is an engineering standard. Service made it instinct.

Credential

RCDD

BICSI
Registered Communications Distribution Designer

The RCDD is BICSI's flagship credential for ICT systems designers. It validates expertise in the design and integration of communications systems, data centers, security systems, and the associated limited energy infrastructure. Holders demonstrate competency across telecommunications distribution, structured cabling, and building systems coordination.
