FINANCIAL ROLL-UP
  • Schedule-driven crew sizing. Total scope hours / (hours per day × target working days), rounded up. Larger crew = shorter calendar; baseline-crew callout flags acceleration.
  • OT math (federal). Hours above 40 / tech / week earn the OT multiplier. Premium = OT_hours × avg field rate × (OT_mult − 1).
  • OT math (California). Greater of (daily OT: hours per day above 8) or (weekly OT: hours per week above 40). Double-time triggers when hours per day exceed 12. 7th-consecutive-day rule not modeled in v1.0.
  • Vehicle adder. Total field labor hours × per-hour adder. Represents truck/tools/fuel amortization.
  • Remote ODC auto-populate. Per diem = crew × (target days + travel days) × rate. Hotel = crew × nights × rate. Travel = crew × airfare. Lines surface in the ODC panel and the summary as auto-computed; manual ODC lines add on top.
  • Overhead. Applied as % of direct costs (labor + materials + ODC).
  • Margin form. Final price = (direct × (1 + overhead)) / (1 − margin%). Margin form, not naive markup — ensures the stated margin is the actual margin on the proposal price.
  • BICSI WBS. Section codes 000–1000 are pre-created on every new estimate. Empty sections contribute 0 to totals; toggle “Hide empty sections” to declutter the Builder view.
  • Scope. Preliminary estimation only. Verify against shop standards before issuing a sealed proposal.

CORE FORMULAS

1. Per WBS item:

labor_hours = qty × hours_each
labor_cost = labor_hours × role_rate
materials_cost = mat_qty × mat_unit_cost
line_total = labor_cost + materials_cost

2. Per section: sum of line items.

3. Project totals: sum of sections + manual ODC + auto-populated remote ODC.

4. OT premium:

ot_hours_total = crew_size × ot_hrs_per_tech_per_wk × weeks
ot_premium = ot_hours_total × avg_field_rate × (ot_mult − 1)

5. Final price (margin form):

subtotal = direct_costs × (1 + overhead%)
final_price = subtotal / (1 − margin%)

800 Ancillary — misc. items not covered above; sleeve seal kits, miscellaneous labor.

1000 Demobilization — cleanup, punch list, closeout deliverables, demob labor.

MARGIN FORM vs. NAIVE MARKUP

Naive markup adds a percentage to cost (e.g. cost × 1.20 = “20% markup”). But the actual margin on that price is only ~16.7% (profit / sell), not 20%.

Margin form divides instead: price = cost / (1 − margin%). A 20% margin form on $100 of cost = $125 price, where margin is $25 = 20% of $125. The stated margin equals the actual proposal margin.

The Estimator uses margin form throughout. Enter the target margin you want on the sell price.

SCHEDULE & CREW EXPLAINED

Total scope hours are summed from every WBS item across all sections (labor qty × hours each).


Target working days is your input. The estimator computes the crew size required to hit that target:
crew = ceil(total_hours / (hours_per_day × target_days))


If the required crew exceeds your baseline (default 4 techs), the schedule is flagged as accelerated. You may also want to bump the workweek pattern to 5×10 or 6×8, which triggers overtime math automatically.


Remote jobs auto-populate per-diem / hotel / travel ODC lines from crew size + target days.

OVERHEAD & MARGIN

Overhead % is applied to direct costs (labor + materials + ODC). Typical: 10–20% for non-union shops.


Margin % uses the margin form: price = subtotal / (1 − margin%)


A 20% margin on $100 cost = $125 price ($25 / $125 = 20%). Naive 20% markup ($100 × 1.20 = $120) only yields a 16.7% margin.


Set the margin you want to land on; the estimator does the inverse math.

MATERIALS & LIBRARY

Library is your shop’s catalog of materials. It lives in this browser only (localStorage) — export it often to back up.


Pick from Library opens a browser to add items into your current project. After picking, set quantities in the Materials list.


Import CSV reads a CSV file (e.g. a supplier quote) and adds rows to your library. If a CSV row has qty > 0, the item is also added to the current project with that quantity.


CSV columns (all optional except description): category, description, manufacturer, model, partNumber, sourcePrice, sourceYear, unit, hoursEach, defaultRole, qty, notes. Header row is required; column order doesn’t matter (case-insensitive).


Effective price = source price × project inflation factor. Edit the source price to change pricing.

INFLATION FACTOR

The seed library carries 2023 baseline prices. The inflation factor multiplies those source prices at display and calc time to give a ballpark current dollar.


Default 1.10 represents ~3 years of cumulative inflation (2023 → 2026). Set to 1.0 to disable inflation. Set higher if you’re estimating for a future year or a high-volatility commodity period.


For known-current quotes (supplier just sent you a 2026 sheet), edit the source price directly — or set sourceYear in the CSV to the quote year and adjust the factor accordingly.
