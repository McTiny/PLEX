Design Philosophy

Local-First. Always.

Every LEX design is built to run in your home, on your hardware — whether the internet is up or not.

Cloud services are optional integrations — not the foundation. The dominant smart home model is built on vendor clouds: your device checks in with a server in another state, gets its instruction, and comes back. That server can go down. That company can be acquired. That product can be discontinued. Your home ends up holding the bag.

We design around a local hub running on hardware you own. Automations execute locally. Sensors report locally. The internet going out doesn't mean your lights stop responding. You're not dependent on a company's uptime or product roadmap to live in your own home.

No monthly fees. No vendor lock-in. No surprise deprecations. The system we design today runs the same way five years from now. Your investment is in hardware and design — not a recurring subscription that evaporates when you cancel.

Platform Coverage

Systems We Work With

Chosen for reliability, local capability, and field-proven performance — not marketing.

Climate Control
HVAC & Climate

Modern HVAC systems — variable speed, heat pump, multi-zone — generate a rich data stream. Where a manufacturer allows integration, we capture that data and put it to work: temperature history, runtime analysis, efficiency trends, and real-time alerts. No thermostat swap required. Platform: Resideo / Honeywell Home.

Z-Wave · Open Protocol
Door & Window Contacts

Z-Wave is our primary contact sensor protocol: no cloud dependency, encrypted mesh, and battery life measured in years. We design for scale — 25+ sensors in a single system isn't uncommon. Budget-conscious options bring per-unit cost down to around $15 without sacrificing reliability.

Ring · Preferred Platform
Security Cameras

Ring is our preferred camera platform for its proven integration, wide product line, and field-proven reliability. We design motion automations, doorbell alerts, and notification routing as part of every security scope. Budget alternatives are available where applicable.

YoLink · LoRa Mesh
Leak & Water Detection

YoLink is our preferred leak detection platform — long range, local MQTT capable, and purpose-built for sensors that have to work every single time. Garage, HVAC drain pan, laundry, bathrooms — every risk point covered. YoLink's LoRa protocol reaches where Wi-Fi doesn't.

Samsung SmartThings
Appliances

We work with Samsung SmartThings as a compatible integration layer. Your washer, dryer, and refrigerator aren't just appliances — they're nodes in your automation system. Left the fridge door open? You'll know. Laundry done? You'll know that too.

ntfy · Self-Hosted
Notifications

We use ntfy as the notification backbone — self-hosted, fast, and free. Every alert your system generates routes through a single, privacy-respecting push notification stack. No third-party apps required. No ads. No data harvesting.

Aladdin Connect
Garage Access

Aladdin Connect provides open/close state, last-event timestamps, and full automation integration. Know when the garage opened, who triggered it, and set automations that close it automatically at a set time or when the house enters an away state.

Tesla Powerwall
Energy Monitoring

Tesla Powerwall integration surfaces grid power, solar production, battery charge state, and per-circuit load data through Grafana dashboards backed by InfluxDB time-series storage. Real-time energy intelligence: generation vs. consumption, cost tracking, and historical trend analysis.

TP-Link Kasa
Lighting

Kasa smart switches and plugs operate entirely on the local LAN — no cloud account needed for automation. Fast response, reliable scheduling, and full integration with the home automation hub via Node.js services on the local network.

Live energy dashboards — Powerwall charge state, solar production, grid draw, battery flow, and real-time cost tracking against TXU Solar Buyback Plus.

In Practice

The Home Automation Stack

These systems aren't theoretical. They run on a dedicated on-premise home server that powers both the private tooling stack and the home automation platform for this residence.

Home Assistant with Lovelace UI is the automation hub — handling device states, automations, scenes, and presence detection. The UI is fully custom: JSON and HTML configuration throughout, with every dashboard panel purpose-built for the actual use case rather than a generic template.

Node.js integration services bridge manufacturer APIs directly — Kasa lighting, YoLink sensor data via MQTT, SmartThings appliance states, Resideo thermostat, Tesla Powerwall energy data, and Aladdin Connect garage access. No cloud middleware. Data flows from device to local service to hub.

Grafana dashboards backed by InfluxDB provide time-series visibility across the whole system — energy production and consumption (grid/solar/battery), environmental sensors (temperature, humidity, air quality), appliance states, and security events. The energy dashboard tracks per-session cost, daily kWh totals, and Powerwall charge percentage in real time.

Tailscale VPN provides secure remote access to the entire stack — monitoring and control from anywhere, with no ports exposed to the public internet.
