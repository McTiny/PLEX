Limited Energy eXperts (LEX) is an independent consulting practice focused on practical AI adoption for engineering, limited energy design, and home automation workflows. LEX helps turn repetitive technical work into structured, repeatable processes — from Division 27 and Division 28 design support to AI-assisted estimating, documentation, coordination, and system planning.


The focus is not AI for its own sake. It is using AI where it actually helps: reducing manual effort, improving consistency, accelerating design decisions, and giving engineers, contractors, and homeowners better tools to solve real problems.

What I'm Building

Tools

The free tools on this site — The Dude and The Estimator — came from real project needs. The Dude was built to simplify telecom room power, UPS, generator, and cooling coordination. The Estimator was built because limited energy bidding still depends too heavily on spreadsheets that were never designed for Division 27/28 scope. The remaining tools run on a private local stack.

Each agent performs 90–95% of the lift — intake, parsing, comparison, draft output. The engineer of record reviews, owns, and stands behind the result. The judgment calls stay with the human.

Your helpful guide for PLEX Power Load EXaminer

Purpose-built utility for sizing telecom room and equipment room infrastructure. Input active equipment loads and The Dude calculates power distribution requirements, UPS sizing, generator sizing, PoE budget, and cooling demand — aligned to BICSI and TIA standards.

The Estimator
BICSI WBS  ·  Limited Energy Job Costing  ·  Customer Proposal & Internal Bid Report

Client-side job estimator purpose-built for limited energy contractors. Builds from BICSI WBS section codes, calculates labor using configurable crew roles, workweek patterns, and federal or California OT rules. Generates sell-side pricing using margin form, produces a customer-ready proposal, and an internal bid report with financial roll-up, bid health indicators, and auto-generated risk flags. No account required. No data leaves the browser.

Perspective

AI in Div 27 / 28 Design

How this tooling gets built and why it is being built this way.

The limited energy design space has a documentation problem. Specifications get recycled from project to project, BOMs drift from drawings, and the practitioners with the most domain knowledge spend a disproportionate amount of time on formatting, cross-referencing, and administrative output that adds no design value.

A model that understands TIA-568, knows the difference between a TR and an ER, and can generate Section 270000 language from a design intent conversation is a force multiplier. It still requires the credentialed practitioner who has to review it, own it, and stand behind it. The RCDD drives. The AI handles the repetitive field work.

Everything runs locally on dedicated on-premise hardware — a private inference and home automation server I own and operate. No cloud inference. No third-party data exposure. Sensitive project information stays where it belongs. The stack is built on open-weight models grounded in real standards language, not generalist models that hallucinate BICSI references and invent TIA clause numbers. Secure remote access via Tailscale keeps the stack off the public internet entirely.
