POWER FACTOR EXPLAINED

Power Factor (PF) is the ratio of real power (watts — what does work and generates heat) to apparent power (volt-amps — what the utility delivers and UPS must supply).


Example: A 500W server at PF 0.90 draws 556 VA from the UPS and circuit. Size UPS and breakers to VA, not watts.


Typical values:
· Modern servers with active PFC: 0.95–0.99
· Network switches: 0.90–0.95
· Older or mixed IT loads: 0.80–0.90
· Conservative design default: 0.90
