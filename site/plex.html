POWER CONSUMPTION & CIRCUIT SCHEDULE
  • Power Factor. Default 0.90 applied to all loads. Adjust under Global Settings for known equipment behavior.
  • NEC 125% Continuous Load. Applied for planning — breaker and generator sizing. Final circuiting to be verified and signed off by EOR/AHJ.
  • Wall-Mount Device Wattages. Wattages for wall-mounted devices (ACS controllers, power supplies, locks, intercoms, and field equipment) are reference estimates. Verify actual draw against manufacturer specifications and final hardware selections before design completion.
  • Cooling Load. BTU/hr = Watts × 3.412. Cooling tons = BTU/hr ÷ 12,000. HVAC recommendation includes a 25% buffer.
  • Generator Sizing. Total load × 1.25 (continuous). Growth recommendations show +25% and +50% above continuous.
  • UPS Runtime. Linear estimate from nameplate full-load runtime, scaled by load percentage. Actual runtime varies with battery age, temperature, and load profile.
  • Breaker Selection. Breaker shown is the next standard commercial size at or above calculated continuous-load current. Final breaker and conductor sizing by licensed electrical engineer.
  • Redundant Power Supplies. Total system draw is unchanged — dual PSU equipment shares the load between supplies.
  • Scope. Preliminary planning and coordination only. PLEX does not replace stamped electrical design. All outputs require review and sign-off by a licensed electrical engineer.

POWER FACTOR EXPLAINED

Power Factor (PF) is the ratio of real power (watts — what does work and generates heat) to apparent power (volt-amps — what the utility delivers and UPS must supply).


Example: A 500W server at PF 0.90 draws 556 VA from the UPS and circuit. Size UPS and breakers to VA, not watts.


Typical values:
· Modern servers with active PFC: 0.95–0.99
· Network switches: 0.90–0.95
· Older or mixed IT loads: 0.80–0.90
· Conservative design default: 0.90

AMBIENT HEAT GAIN

Additional heat load added to the cooling calculation to account for lighting, room envelope, and occasional personnel. This is applied on top of the IT equipment BTU load before the BICSI/ASHRAE 1.25× planning buffer.


Typical values:
· Minimal interior TR with no exterior walls: 5%
· Standard interior TR/ER: 10% (default)
· ER with exterior exposure or lighting load: 15%
· Worst-case / conservative planning: 20%


This field affects the cooling calculation only — it does not change electrical load, breaker sizing, or generator output.

UPS PLANNING SETTINGS

Demand Factor — ratio of actual operating load to connected nameplate load. Use 1.00 for conservative design; lower for known partial loads.


Growth Factor — multiplier for future load expansion. Default 1.25 = 25% growth. Recommended for 5–10 year planning horizon.


Max Load % Target — maximum UPS loading at planning load. 80% is standard; 75% for mission-critical. UPS will be sized so planning load does not exceed this threshold.


Inverter Efficiency — UPS inverter efficiency for runtime calculation. Typical: 90–96% for double-conversion. Check manufacturer specs.


Runtime Target — minimum desired runtime in minutes at planning load. Used to verify battery capacity and flag shortfalls.
